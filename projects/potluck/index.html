KARAK TEA

Creamy, bold, and deeply spiced, karak tea is the Middle Eastern cousin of chai, known for its rich flavors and comforting warmth. Infused with cardamom, cinnamon, and ginger, it's simmered slowly to create a velvety, fragrant cup. Perfect for chilly mornings or whenever you need a little extra warmth, this tea never disappoints.

PRINT RECIPE PIN RECIPE

PREP TIME: 5 MINUTES

COOK TIME: 10 MINUTES

TOTAL TIME: 15 MINUTES

YIELDS: 4

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups water
  • 2 cups evaporated milk
  • 6 to 8 cardamom pods, lightly crushed
  • 3 cinnamon sticks
  • 1 ginger cube (or a small piece of fresh ginger)
  • 1/4 cup light brown sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 4 black/red tea bags

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a medium pot, combine the water, evaporated milk, cardamom pods, cinnamon sticks, and ginger. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil over medium heat.

  2. Once it starts bubbling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 5 to 7 minutes. This allows the spices to infuse the milk and water with their rich, warm flavors.

  3. Place the tea bags into the pot and let them steep for 3 to 5 minutes, depending on how strong you like your tea. Stir occasionally to help the tea blend with the spiced milk. You'll know it's ready when the color turns a deep, caramel brown. Stir in the brown sugar until it dissolves completely.

  4. Add the vanilla extract and let it simmer for another minute to enhance the flavor.

  5. Remove the pot from the heat and carefully strain the tea into cups to remove the spices and tea bags.

  6. Serve hot and enjoy!

TIRAMISU LATTE

If you love tiramisu, this latte will easily become your fave way to caffeinate! Sweet espresso, creamy milk, and a velvety mascarpone foam come together for a sip that's pure indulgence. Whether hot or iced, it's like treating yourself to dessert—but in drinkable form!

PREP TIME: 10 MINUTES

TOTAL TIME: 10 MINUTES

YIELDS: 2

INGREDIENTS

    FOR THE ESPRESSO:
  • 4 shots espresso
  • 2 to 4 tablespoons brown sugar or chocolate mocha sauce
    SWEET FOAM:
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup mascarpone cheese
  • 2 tablespoons simple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    FOR THE LATTE:
  • 1 to 2 cups milk (whole milk or preferred milk)
  • ice (for iced version)
  • cocoa powder or chocolate shavings (optional, for garnish)
  • lady fingers, optional

INSTRUCTIONS

    PREPARE ESPRESSO:
  1. Brew 4 shots of espresso, 2 per drink. For a stronger drink, brew 3 shots of espresso per drink.

  2. While still hot, stir in brown sugar or mocha sauce to your desired sweetness until fully dissolved. Set aside.

    3. MAKE TIRAMISU FOAM:
  3. In a mixing bowl or using a handheld frother, combine 1/4 cup heavy cream, 2 tablespoons mascarpone cheese, 1 tablespoon simple syrup, and 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract.

  4. Whip until the mixture thickens slightly, forming a soft, pourable foam.

    5. HOT LATTE:
  5. Heat 1 cup of milk until steaming, but do not let it boil. Froth if desired.

  6. Pour the sweetened espresso into a mug.

  7. Add the steamed milk, filling the mug almost to the top.

  8. Spoon the sweet foam generously over the top.

  9. Sprinkle with cocoa powder or chocolate shavings if desired.
    10. ICED LATTE:
  10. Fill a glass with ice.

  11. Pour 1 cup of cold milk over the ice, leaving room for the espresso.

  12. Slowly pour the sweetened espresso over the milk to create a layered effect.

  13. Top with the sweet foam, letting it cascade over the ice.

  14. Finish with a dusting of cocoa powder or chocolate shavings if desired.

HOT COCOA WITH TORCHED MARSHMALLOW TOPPING

This hot cocoa is the ultimate cozy treat, topped with a luscious swirl of marshmallow that's toasted to golden perfection. the combination of rich, creamy cocoa and the slightly crisp, fluffy topping feels like a warm hug in a mug. It's indulgent, fun, and perfect for any chilly day!

PRINT RECIPE PIN RECIPE

PREP TIME: 10 MINUTES

COOK TIME: 20 MINUTES

TOTAL TIME: 30 MINUTES

YIELDS: 4

INGREDIENTS

    HOT COCOA:
    • 1 3/4 cup evaporated milk
    • 1 1/2 cup whole or 2% milk
    • 1/4 cup heavy cream
    • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
    • 4 ounces semi-sweet chocolate
    • pinch of salt
    MARSHMALLOW TOPPING:
    • 4 egg whites
    • 1 cup white granulated sugar
    • 1 cup corn syrup
    • 1/2 cup water
    • 3/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

    INSTRUCTIONS

    PREPARE THE HOT COCOA:
  1. In a medium saucepan, combine evaporated milk, regular milk, and heavy cream over medium heat.

  2. Stir in brown sugar, cocoa powder, and a pinch of salt until smooth and warmed through.

  3. Add semi-sweet chocolate and whisk until fully melted.

  4. Stir in vanilla extract, adjust sweetness as desired, and keep warm on low heat.

    5. MAKE THE MARSHMALLOW FLUFF:
  5. In a mixing bowl, add egg whites and cream of tartar. Whisk until the mixture becomes bubbly and foamy.

  6. In a saucepan, combine water, sugar, and corn syrup. Heat over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves.

  7. Allow the mixture to simmer without stirring further, until it reaches about 240°F or 115°C using a candy thermometer.

  8. With the mixer running at medium speed, slowly stream the hot syrup into the egg whites. Avoid pouring too quickly to prevent scrambling.

  9. Once all the syrup is added, increase the mixer speed to high and beat until the mixture is glossy and holds stiff peaks.

    10. ASSEMBLE AND TORCH:
  10. Pour the prepared hot cocoa into cups or mugs.

  11. Transfer the marshmallow topping to a piping bag fitted with a star or round tip.

  12. Pipe the marshmallow around the rim of each cup.

  13. Use a kitchen torch to toast the marshmallow until golden brown.

  14. Serve immediately and enjoy your elevated hot cocoa experience!
